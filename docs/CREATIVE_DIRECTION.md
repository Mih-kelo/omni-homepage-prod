# LUMEN — The Clarity Engine

### Creative direction for the Omni Target flagship digital experience

| | |
|---|---|
| **Client** | Omni Target — pre-spend intelligence for Shopify fashion brands |
| **Engagement** | Flagship digital experience · US $500,000 · 26 weeks |
| **Horizon** | Built to hold for five years |
| **Document** | Treatment v1.0 · July 2026 |
| **Contains** | Narrative, interaction, and aesthetic direction only. No code, by design. |

**How to read this document.** Section 3 is the script — read it like a screenplay. Section 4 is the shot list for the fourteen moments that must be unforgettable. Sections 5 through 7 are the systems that keep the world alive. Everything is described, nothing is implemented; the build team owns the *how*, this document owns the *what it must feel like*.

---

## 1 · Manifesto

Every software website describes intelligence. This one behaves intelligently.

Omni Target's product promise is a specific and unusual one: **clarity before spend**. It reads a Shopify store the way a great media buyer would — orders, products, customers, collections, all measured against the store's own baseline — and hands back a complete Meta brief in three minutes, before a single advertising dollar leaves the building. The website must deliver that same feeling — *someone extremely competent just looked at my store* — within eight seconds of arrival, and it must do it without asking anyone to read a paragraph.

The audience makes the bar higher. Fashion and lifestyle operators judge taste instantly and unforgivingly. They live in lookbooks, ateliers, and editorial pages, not dashboards. A site that looks like every venture-backed SaaS product — dark hero, floating screenshot, three-column feature grid — tells them this tool was not built for people like them. So we don't build a website. We build an **instrument they walk through**: part optics laboratory, part couture atelier, part kinetic sculpture, in which their own kind of commerce — garments, buyers, seasons — visibly becomes light, passes through a living intelligence, and lands as a finished campaign.

Four rules govern every decision in this document:

1. **Light over dark.** The world is a white atelier. Darkness is an event, not a default.
2. **Motion over copy.** Twelve narrative lines carry the entire argument. Everything else is shown.
3. **Evidence over adjectives.** No superlatives. The machine demonstrates; placards state numbers.
4. **One continuous shot.** The visitor never changes pages, never watches a section reset. They travel.

And one test, applied ruthlessly: **if any frame of this experience could be screenshotted and mistaken for another SaaS website, that frame is cut.**

## 2 · The World

### The metaphor: commerce is light

A Shopify store throws off thousands of tiny signals a day — an order at 2 a.m., a garment viewed nine times and never bought, a customer who returns every drop. Scattered, these signals illuminate nothing. Gathered and focused, they tell an operator exactly what to do next.

So in this world, **data behaves as light**. Store events are luminous motes drifting in white space. The AI is a living glass Core that gathers scattered light and refracts it into order. Campaigns are focused beams that travel down a long hall and strike targets. The brand name was always the brief: **Omni** — light from everywhere. **Target** — focused to a point. The entire experience is the journey of light from *everywhere* to *one point*, and pre-spend intelligence is dramatized literally: **the machine aims before it fires.**

### The place: a white atelier-observatory

Not outer space. Not cyberspace. Not a void with a neon grid. The world is a *place* with air in it — a sequence of white chambers washed in perpetual morning studio light, connected like the halls of one enormous instrument. Its materials are the materials of the audience's own world: paper, glass, frosted acrylic, hairline metal, archival photography. The machine that lives inside it is warm and slightly imperfect — glass that breathes, light that drifts — never robotic, never anthropomorphic. No faces, no chat bubbles, no eyes.

### The camera: one unbroken move

The whole experience is a single dolly shot. Scroll does not move a page; it moves a **camera along a hand-authored path** through the instrument. Chambers are regions of one continuous world, not sections of a document. Nothing resets, nothing re-enters from the side, nothing fades in for decoration. When the world darkens for the two cinematic passages, the visitor *travels into* the darkness and *travels out* of it — there is no cut in the entire piece.

### What we never show

Browser windows. Dashboards. Floating laptops and phones. Screenshots of the product. Stock humans smiling at laptops. Cursor-clicks-the-UI demo videos. The product's *output* appears instead as physical artifacts — glass specimen slides, a bound brief, figures engraved in a wall — because artifacts photograph like objects of desire and screenshots photograph like software.

## 3 · The Journey

Eight chambers, one camera move. Percentages are shares of total scroll length. At a reading pace the journey runs six to eight minutes; a fast scroller gets a coherent ninety-second cut (see §5, Scroll grammar). Each chamber is scripted as: **The room · The action · Your hand** (what the visitor's cursor can do) **· The line** (the wall text) **· The exit** (how it becomes the next chamber).

### Chamber 0 — The Threshold — *curiosity* (7%)

**The room.** Warm white, edge to edge. A field of thousands of light motes drifts in slow currents — close ones soft and dimensional, far ones atmospheric. Each mote is a commerce event: drift near one and a small mono label resolves beside it — *order · 02:14* — *product view · ninth visit* — *returning customer*. The field breathes as one body, roughly the rhythm of calm human breath.

**The action.** The page's own loading is the opening scene (Moment 1, *The First Breath*). Once the field settles, a single serif line rises out of it. Below, one small mono sub-line. Nothing else — no headline stack, no subheadline paragraph, no picture of anything.

**Your hand.** The Lensing Cursor (Moment 2) is established here in the first second of contact: motes lean gently toward the cursor like dust toward static, and the first thing the visitor learns — before reading a word — is *this place notices me*.

**The line.** “Your store already knows.” — and beneath it, mono: *Omni Target · pre-spend intelligence for Shopify fashion brands.*

**The instrument panel.** The only persistent interface: a slender frosted-glass strip that materializes on first cursor movement. It carries the wordmark, a hairline thread of light showing position along the journey, a sound toggle, a “Still” toggle (§10), and one action — **Open the instrument** — which is the only route to the app until the final chamber. There is no menu; the journey is the menu. A small index affordance unfolds a map of the chambers for returning visitors and keyboard travelers.

**The exit.** The first scroll tips the camera forward and slightly down. The drifting motes ahead begin to align into currents — the chaos, it turns out, has a direction — and the void acquires perspective. Curiosity is converted into motion.

### Chamber 1 — The Listening — *wonder* (12%)

**The room.** The white opens into a long gallery where the first glass structures of the machine appear: slender conduits, frosted vanes, all catching the light. Ahead, a small warm node — the store. Abstract, tasteful; a seed of commerce, not a logo lockup.

**The action.** As the camera approaches, a single indigo thread reaches from the machine and finds the store node (Moment 3, *The Thread*). On contact, the thread splits into four tributaries that ignite one by one, each labeled in small mono type: *orders · products · customers · collections*. Light begins to flow. Riding the product tributary come the garments themselves — translucent **glass specimen slides**, each carrying a piece of archival-style fashion photography and a shimmer of metadata, schooling like fish (Moment 4, *The Shoal*). A faint horizontal hairline travels with the streams, labeled *your store's baseline* — every signal rides visibly above or below it. The analysis is relative, and the visitor can see that it is.

**Your hand.** Hovering the shoal parts it. The chosen slide banks out of formation, swims to the camera, and tilts to face the visitor as its metadata resolves into legibility — *first-time buyers · repeat rate · cold-traffic history*. Release, and it rejoins the school. It is the product demo nobody will call a demo.

**The line.** “One thread. Read-only. Three minutes.”

**The exit.** The four tributaries converge, bend downward together like water finding a channel, and pour toward a brighter room below. The camera banks and follows the light.

### Chamber 2 — The Paradox Gallery — *intrigue, then insight* (13%)

The intellectual heart of the piece. This chamber exists to make the visitor *learn something true about their own business* inside one minute — because nothing builds belief in an intelligence faster than being taught by it.

**The room.** A bright gallery with two pedestals, staged like a museum comparison. On the left, under a warm **gold** halo — the only gold in the entire experience — stands a garment slide on a gilded mount: the bestseller. It is magnificent and it is mobbed: a dense orbit of warm motes circles it. On the right, in flat unlit white: a quiet, unremarkable slide. The understudy.

**The action.** Drift closer to the bestseller and its mono placard tells the uncomfortable truth: *first-time buyers 12% · average order $640 · cold-traffic return 0.6×*. The adoring orbit, on inspection, is labeled *returning customers* — loyalists it already owns. Then the scroll crosses the threshold and the machine acts (Moment 5, *The Understudy*): a spotlight swings — a slow, gorgeous sweep of light across the white floor — off the gilded piece and onto the quiet one, which ignites. Cool indigo motes — *strangers* — stream in from outside the frame and rush to it. Its placard resolves: *first-time buyers 64% · average order $110 · cold-traffic return 2.4×*. The gold halo, meanwhile, cools to grey.

**Your hand.** The two orbits are live: the cursor's gravity can draw a few motes off course, but the returning-customer orbit always falls back to the bestseller, and the stranger stream always finds the gateway. The system is teaching its thesis through physics.

**The line.** “Your bestseller keeps customers. Your gateway product makes them.” — and in small curator's italic beneath: *The product that converts your existing audience is rarely the one that earns you a new one.*

**The exit.** The gateway slide lifts from its pedestal and glides ahead of the camera, escorted by its new crowd, toward an aperture at the end of the gallery where the light narrows and deepens. The white walls fall away by degrees — the descent into the Core has already begun, and no one saw a transition happen.

### Chamber 3 — The Core — *awe* (15%) — dark passage one

**The room.** The world dims the way a theater dims — continuously, in travel — until the visitor floats in deep indigo-black. Suspended at the center: the **Core**, a glass polyhedron the size of a room, slightly asymmetric, organic rather than machined, veined with slow interior light. It rotates almost imperceptibly. It breathes on a six-second period. It was working before the visitor arrived, and it will be working after they leave.

**The action.** The four tributaries arrive from behind the camera and spiral inward; the gateway slide leads them in. The Core inhales — contracts a fraction — and the chamber executes the thesis of the entire company in about eight seconds, with zero copy (Moment 6, *The Assembly*): chaos in, order out. Refractions cascade through the interior veins, and from the far face emerge ordered artifacts in a calm procession — an audience ring, a set of creative frames, a written hook, a budget arc, an optimization dial — each snapping onto a clean orbital path.

**Your hand.** The visitor can interfere. Cursor movement bends the inbound streams off course — and the Core *compensates*, visibly re-routing light around the disturbance and producing the same ordered output. Robustness is character: the machine is unbothered by you, and that is precisely what makes it feel intelligent rather than scripted.

**The line.** Before entry, small and alone in the dark: “Three minutes happen here.” Nothing else. Darkness needs no copy.

**The exit.** The camera does not go around the Core. It goes **through** it (Moment 7, *Through the Glass*): a full-screen refraction, one heartbeat of pure indigo, and the world reassembles into the brightest white of the piece on the far side. This transit *is* the dark-to-light transition — nothing switches, the visitor travels through the machine's own glass and comes out the other side.

### Chamber 4 — The Composition Hall — *understanding* (18%)

The longest chamber, because this is where the product becomes concrete. The five artifacts that emerged from the Core dock, one by one, into a composition — the brief being *set*, like a page being typeset at architectural scale. Five beats:

**Beat one — the audience.** A single indigo point — the store's actual first-time-buyer profile — splits into a thousand points that arrange themselves into concentric orbital rings around the gateway product (Moment 8, *The Audience Bloom*). Rings carry quiet mono labels: age band, interest clusters, lookalike depth. Audience construction rendered as astronomy.

**Beat two — the creative.** A creative prescription assembles itself in mid-air (Moment 9, *The Living Canvas*): four carousel frames self-build — photography develops like a print in a bath, a headline typesets letter by letter and settles with a typographic snap, a call-to-action docks with a soft magnetic click. Then the finished canvas fans into ranked variants like dealt cards, each labeled with its conversion likelihood. Format, angle, and ranking — shown, not listed.

**Beat three — the hooks.** A stylus of light writes across the air in serif italic: *“The one piece that quietly outsells everything.”* Beneath it, a mono footnote: *derived from repeat-purchase behavior · no reviews required.* The machine writes the way the brand would speak.

**Beat four — the money.** A horizontal glass channel fills with liquid indigo and stops, precisely, at *$42 a day*. A hairline mark further along the channel reads *scale at 1.8× return*. Beside it, a physical dial clicks one notch to *optimize: purchases · 7-day click*. Budget as liquid, calibrated to the store's real order volume — visibly not a template number.

**Beat five — the artifact.** Everything contracts (Moment 11, *The Binding*): the constellation, the canvas, the hook, the channel and dial all typeset themselves onto pages of light, the pages gather, square up, and bind into **the Brief** — a slim, embossed document turning slowly in the air, the store's own name stamped into its cover. This object is what a customer actually buys, and the experience treats it with the reverence of a couture piece.

**Your hand.** Each beat is disturbable — sway the constellation, tilt a canvas, ripple the liquid — and each recovers with grace. Touch teaches structure.

**The line.** “A complete brief. Nothing missing.” — mono, below: *generated in 4.2 seconds · exported as PDF.*

**The exit.** The Brief glides forward, and the hall's far wall opens into the longest perspective of the piece: a white range, stretching away.

### Chamber 5 — The Range — *confidence* (12%)

**The room.** A long white hall — an archery range crossed with a camera obscura. At its far end, faint target rings. Between here and there: clean air and distance. This is where “pre-spend” stops being a word and becomes an image.

**The action.** A beam extends from the Brief and begins to **deliberate**: it sweeps the distant rings slowly, brightening over one, dimming over another, passing entirely over a third — visible aim, visible judgment, money not yet spent. Then the scroll crosses the commit point and the beam fires — one clean indigo line down the hall, a soft bloom on impact — and the bloom's energy engraves the wall (Moment 10, *The Ledger of Light*): a rising revenue curve etched in hairline, and two figures in oversized editorial serif — *2.4× cold-traffic return · 64% first-time buyers* — presented like inscriptions on a museum placard, not widgets on a dashboard.

Beside the figures, small and matter-of-fact, the honesty placard: *Numbers from a pilot brand. Fashion, Shopify-native, under NDA.* Specific, restrained, and therefore believable.

**Your hand.** Scroll velocity feeds the beam's charge — patient visitors watch the deliberation in full; fast ones still see aim-then-fire, never fire-then-aim. The order of those two events is the entire product and it is never allowed to invert.

**The line.** “Aim before you spend.”

**The exit.** The camera lifts. The hall's walls sink away beneath it, and the ceiling opens into a dim vault of air — ascent, not cut.

### Chamber 6 — The Observatory — *trust* (8%) — dark passage two

**The room.** High and dark and quiet. And below — the reveal (Moment 12, *The Overview Effect*): the **entire instrument at once**. Threshold, tributaries, gallery, Core, hall, range — one continuous glass organism, still flowing, still breathing, still processing. Everything the visitor just walked through was one machine, and it never stopped moving while they weren't looking.

The traces persist. The slide they hovered in the Shoal still carries a faint warmth. The constellation they swayed is still settling. The machine remembered them — quietly, without a popup asking permission for the metaphor.

Around the instrument, at great distance, other instruments glow as faint constellations: other stores, other briefs, the sense of a working network — conveyed without a single fake logo strip or live counter.

**Your hand.** The whole miniature is lensed: the cursor's gravity sways the entire organism gently, one system, one body.

**The line.** “It's been listening the whole time. It always is.” — and etched small, the audience placard: *Built for fashion & lifestyle operators on Shopify, $5k–$100k a month. Not for B2B. Not for autopilot.* Stating who it is not for is a confidence only real products have.

**The exit.** The camera descends, and light rises around it like dawn arriving early — back into white, brighter than before.

### Chamber 7 — The Invitation — *desire, then action* (15%)

**The room.** The purest white in the piece, emptier than the Threshold. Nearly everything is gone. At center hangs a single object — **the Lens**: a disc of deep indigo glass, the only saturated thing on screen.

**The action.** Every ambient mote in the world drifts, slowly and unmistakably, toward the Lens (Moment 13, *The Gravity Well*). The visitor's own cursor feeds the drift. Hover, and the Lens focuses — a soft caustic blooms on the white beneath it, light gathered to a point. Press, and before the app opens, a pulse travels **backward through the entire machine** — each chamber flashing gently in sequence into the depth of the scene, Threshold to Range, a farewell running the full length of the instrument. The machine acknowledges the visitor on their way out. Then, and only then: the doorway to the product.

**The offer.** Pricing appears without a pricing section. Four slim glass plates rest in a shallow arc, like large-format film stock in an archive drawer: *Free — 1 brief · Starter $39 — 5 briefs · Growth $99 — 15 briefs · Scale $179 — 30 briefs.* Hover a plate and it lifts, light passing through it to reveal a brief's silhouette inside — you are literally buying these documents. The Growth plate carries a single hairline indigo edge: the recommendation, whispered. One mono line beneath the drawer: *Credits, not subscriptions. Billed through Shopify. Valid twelve months.*

**The line.** “Point it at your store.” — and, as the final placard in small serif: *Shopify knows what sells. Meta knows who buys. Now they talk.*

**The end.** The footer is a colophon: one hairline row — wordmark, privacy, terms, sound toggle — and a single live mote drifting across it, forever. The machine never stops, and the last thing the visitor sees says so.

## 4 · The Fourteen Signature Moments

The shot list. Each card: where it lives, what triggers it, the choreography, why it stays with people, and the line it must not cross — the failure mode that would cheapen it. These fourteen are the memory of the site; everything else exists to connect them.

**1 · The First Breath** — *Threshold · page load.*
The loader is the overture. Arrival begins as a white field of soft blur; as assets stream in, blur condenses into individual motes — loading rendered as light coming into focus. In the final beat the motes drift together, assemble the Omni Target wordmark for roughly a second, and release it back into the field. Cold start to living world in under three seconds on a good connection — and the loading was the show.
*Why it stays:* nobody has ever remembered a progress bar.
*The line it must not cross:* never block a returning visitor — subsequent visits condense in under a second.

**2 · The Lensing Cursor** — *everywhere · continuous.*
The site-wide identity interaction. The cursor is an optical mass: motes lean toward it, connection lines re-route smoothly around it, wall text refracts by a hair's breadth as it passes. Strength varies by chamber — strongest in the Threshold and Invitation, nearly subliminal during reading moments.
*Why it stays:* it is the physical sensation of being noticed by the machine.
*The line:* displacement stays under roughly three percent of any element's size; past that, an instrument becomes a gimmick.

**3 · The Thread** — *The Listening · on approach.*
One indigo filament leaves the machine, crosses the white, and finds the store node; a soft pulse of connection, then the split into four labeled tributaries that ignite in sequence, a half-beat apart. OAuth as a gesture of trust: one thread, visibly thin, visibly read-only.
*Why it stays:* it makes “connect your store” feel like being found rather than like filling in a form.
*The line:* the thread is delicate — never a laser, never a cable.

**4 · The Shoal** — *The Listening · hover.*
Garment slides school with flocking behavior down the product tributary. Hover parts the school around the cursor; the chosen slide banks out, swims close, tilts flat to face the visitor, and its metadata shimmer resolves into type. On release it rejoins the flow, and the school closes behind it.
*Why it stays:* their own world — garments, drops, seasons — swimming through the machine.
*The line:* photography stays archival and flat-lit; the moment the slides look like ads, the poetry dies.

**5 · The Understudy** — *Paradox Gallery · scroll threshold.*
The spotlight swing. Gold halo on the bestseller cools; a slow sweep of light crosses the floor — two full seconds, deliberate, theatrical — and the quiet slide ignites as indigo stranger-motes rush in from off-frame. Numbers resolve on both placards a beat later, letting the light make the argument before the data confirms it.
*Why it stays:* it is the product's core insight staged as theatre, and it teaches the visitor something true about their own store.
*The line:* the bestseller is never mocked or dimmed to black — it remains beautiful. The point is miscasting, not failure.

**6 · The Assembly** — *The Core · scroll-scrubbed set piece.*
The thesis in eight seconds, zero copy. Tributaries spiral in; the Core inhales, flexes; interior refraction cascades; ordered artifacts emerge in procession from the far face and click onto orbital rails. Choreographed as a single musical phrase: gather — hold — release.
*Why it stays:* commerce chaos visibly becomes campaign order, and the visitor was inside it when it happened.
*The line:* the emergence is calm, not explosive. Precision, not fireworks.

**7 · Through the Glass** — *Core exit · scroll.*
The camera enters the Core's surface: refraction sweeps the frame, the world inverts through one heartbeat of pure indigo, and re-forms as the brightest white in the piece. The only full-frame optical event in the experience — and it is diegetic: the visitor passed through the machine's own glass.
*Why it stays:* it is the theme transition as a physical experience; nobody sees a color scheme change, they travel from dark to light.
*The line:* once per journey. Chromatic aberration lives here and nowhere else.

**8 · The Audience Bloom** — *Composition Hall · beat one.*
One point becomes a thousand. The single buyer-profile dot splits in cascading generations, and the points fall into concentric orbital rings around the gateway product — near rings tight and bright (closest lookalikes), far rings wide and faint. Ring labels fade up in mono only after the astronomy settles.
*Why it stays:* “we build lookalike audiences” — rendered as a small galaxy being born around a garment.
*The line:* rings are elliptical and hand-placed, never a perfect radar chart; this is astronomy, not analytics.

**9 · The Living Canvas** — *Composition Hall · beat two.*
Four carousel frames self-assemble mid-air: photograph develops, headline typesets with a settling snap, call-to-action docks with a magnetic click; the finished canvas then fans into ranked A/B variants like a dealt hand of cards, each with its likelihood label.
*Why it stays:* watching an ad build itself is the product demo, and it never shows a pixel of software.
*The line:* the typesetting is typographically perfect — real kerning, real baselines. Sloppy type here would be fatal with this audience.

**10 · The Ledger of Light** — *The Range · beam impact.*
The impact bloom flows into the wall and engraves it: a revenue curve drawn as a single hairline, then two oversized serif figures — 2.4× and 64% — etched like inscriptions. The engraving lags the light by a breath, as if the wall is remembering it.
*Why it stays:* results presented as inscriptions read as history, not as claims.
*The line:* no counting-up number animations. The figures are carved, complete, confident.

**11 · The Binding** — *Composition Hall · finale.*
All five compositions contract onto pages of light; pages gather, square themselves with two crisp typographic snaps, and bind into the Brief — embossed cover, the store's name stamped in blind. It turns slowly, catching light on its edges.
*Why it stays:* it gives the thing customers actually buy — a document — the object-desire of a couture piece.
*The line:* the Brief is matte and physical, never glowing. It is the one object in the world that has left the realm of light and become real.

**12 · The Overview Effect** — *Observatory · reveal.*
Pull back until the whole journey is one visible organism, alive and mid-thought. Session traces persist: the hovered slide still warm, the swayed constellation still settling. Distant constellations imply the other stores this machine serves.
*Why it stays:* comprehension of scale plus the shock of being remembered — the machine kept your fingerprints.
*The line:* traces are subtle warmths, never markers or badges. It remembered you; it doesn't advertise that it did.

**13 · The Gravity Well** — *Invitation · continuous, then press.*
Every ambient mote in the world drifts toward the Lens; cursor movement feeds the current. On hover, focus: a caustic gathers on the white below. On press, the farewell pulse runs backward through every chamber in sequence — the whole instrument lighting up goodbye — and then the app opens.
*Why it stays:* the call to action is a place the entire world has been flowing toward, and pressing it makes the whole journey answer.
*The line:* the pulse runs in under two seconds — a farewell, not a delay before the thing the visitor asked for.

**14 · The Idle Murmur** — *everywhere · after ~20 seconds of stillness.*
The machine never stops. Baseline ambience is permanent: motes drift, glass breathes at a six-second period between roughly 98 and 102 percent scale, distant data-trains cross the deep background every half minute or so. Go idle, and after about twenty seconds the nearest chamber “dreams”: the Core runs a miniature Assembly on its own; the Shoal changes formation; the Range re-aims at a different ring — the machine working because it cannot help itself.
*Why it stays:* stillness is where most sites die. Here, stillness is where the visitor catches the machine being alive when it thinks no one is watching.
*The line:* the murmur never solicits — no bounce, no nudge toward the CTA, no tooltip. It works for itself, which is exactly why it is convincing.

## 5 · Living Systems

The connective tissue that makes the world one organism rather than a reel of effects.

**Presence.** A single model of the visitor — cursor position, scroll velocity, dwell, hover target — feeds every layer at once. The 3D world and the 2D placard layer respond to the same presence, so causality feels unified: hovering a placard brightens the motes behind it; a world event can nudge a placard's light. One nervous system, two bodies.

**The Lensing Cursor, as a system.** A global intensity budget governs reactivity per chamber: high in open chambers (Threshold, Invitation), low during reading beats (placards, pricing), asymptotically zero on functional interface. The lens never fights legibility and never competes with a signature moment that is mid-performance.

**The Idle Murmur, as a system.** Three tiers of ambient life: permanent (drift, breathing, distant trains — never off, even mid-scroll), idle (chamber dreams after ~20 seconds), and deep-idle (after two minutes, the whole world exhales — lighting softens by a step and the breathing deepens — so a visitor returning from another tab finds the place restful, not frozen).

**Memory.** Session-only, in-memory, gone on close: which slide was hovered, which constellation swayed, which chamber held the longest dwell. Spent in exactly one place — the Observatory's persistent traces — because a single quiet proof of memory is worth more than ten features that brag about it. Nothing is tracked beyond the page; the metaphor is honest.

**Scroll grammar.** The journey alternates **travel zones** (camera in motion, world flowing past) and **dwell zones** (camera holds while a set piece performs — the Assembly, the Understudy, the Beam). Scroll velocity is shaped, never fought: fast scrolling compresses travel zones and plays dwell zones in abridged but complete cuts — aim always precedes fire, gather always precedes release. The slow visitor gets a film; the fast visitor gets the trailer; nobody gets an incoherent middle.

## 6 · Aesthetic System

### Palette — light-first, one saturated voice

| Role | Color | Use |
|---|---|---|
| The world | Atelier White #FAFAF8 | Environment, walls, air — the default state of the experience |
| Placards | Paper #F4F2ED | Wall-text surfaces and editorial plates (carried over from the current brand) |
| Ink | Graphite #17171C | All typography in the light world |
| **The voice** | **Deep Indigo #312E81 → #3730A3** | The only saturated color: interactive states, beams, threads, the Core's interior, the Lens |
| The glow | Indigo Light #6366F1 | Refraction highlights and caustics only — never fills, never text |
| Dark passages | Indigo-Black #0B0B1A | The Core and the Observatory — deep indigo darkness, never pure black |
| The irony | Gold #F0C674 | One appearance in the entire experience: the bestseller's halo in the Paradox Gallery — gold as the seductive wrong answer |
| Glass | Frost — whites at low opacity | Slides, plates, conduits, the instrument panel |

The current violet (#6D28D9) retires in favor of the deeper indigo, which reads more editorial and less “tech gradient”; its pale tint may survive only inside refraction highlights. Restraint is the luxury signal: one saturated hue, used like a signature.

### Typography — the editorial voice, evolved

Three voices, inherited from the current brand's DNA and sharpened:

- **The narrator** — a high-contrast editorial serif for wall text and oversized numerals. Playfair Display is the incumbent; candidates for the upgrade pass are GT Sectra, Signifier, or Editorial New — chosen for how their italics perform, since the machine *writes* in italic.
- **The instrument** — a monospaced voice for microlabels and data annotations, 11px, uppercase, wide-tracked. JetBrains Mono is the incumbent; Söhne Mono is the candidate.
- **The clerk** — a quiet grotesk for functional interface only (buttons, toggles, legal). Epilogue may stay; it is never allowed to narrate.

Scale rules: wall text sits large (fluid, roughly 42 to 84px), one line at a time, letter-spaced tight; mono microlabels never exceed their station; numerals in the Ledger and pricing plates are oversized serif — figures as typography, not data.

### Layout & materiality

Museum logic: enormous negative space (placard moments hold a minimum of ~12% viewport side gutters), hairline rules, asymmetric editorial placement — text where a curator would place it, not where a template would. There are no cards, no panels, no rounded-rectangle containers; the only bounded objects in the world are *physical* ones — glass slides, plates, the Brief.

Material vocabulary: glass (transmissive, frosted at edges), paper, hairline metal, light itself. Banned outright: chrome, neon grid floors, lens flares (excepting the two scripted blooms), decorative drop shadows, gradient meshes, glassmorphism-as-decoration (glass here is always an object, never a style), and any texture that would read “sci-fi” rather than “atelier.”

Product imagery: garment photography treated as archive specimens — flat, soft-shadowed, consistent in cast, anonymous enough to feel like *your* store rather than someone else's.

## 7 · Motion & Technology Choreography

How the mandated stack divides the work — described as direction for the build team, not as implementation.

### The three-layer architecture

One **world** (React Three Fiber), one **placard layer** (DOM, directed by Framer Motion), one **conductor** (GSAP) that owns time. The world persists for the entire journey — chambers are regions of a single scene graph, never scenes swapped — which is what makes “no resets, no cuts” physically true rather than aspirational.

### React Three Fiber — the world

- **The mote fields:** tens of thousands of instanced particles driven on the GPU with layered curl-noise drift — the permanent atmosphere. Density, warmth, and current direction are per-chamber parameters, so the same system plays the Threshold's calm, the tributaries' flow, and the Gravity Well's drift.
- **The Core:** a transmission-glass body with interior light veins, breathing via gentle vertex displacement, refracting the world behind it honestly. The single most art-directed object in the piece; it receives its own look-development phase (§12).
- **Streams, beams, and caustics:** curve-following particle ribbons for the tributaries; custom shader work for the beam's deliberation sweep, the impact bloom, and the Lens's gathered caustic.
- **The slides and plates:** textured glass planes with frosted edges — the Shoal's flocking behavior lives here, as does the pricing drawer.
- **The camera:** one hand-authored spline with per-chamber look-at targets and framing. The camera is the narrator; it is animated with the care of a film rig — arrivals ease long, departures pull gently, and it never snaps.
- **The post stack:** subtle bloom (budgeted per chamber), depth of field used editorially — focus pulls between a placard and the world behind it are part of the storytelling — and chromatic aberration permitted only inside Through the Glass. A grading shift carries the warm-white world into the indigo-black passages and back.

### GSAP — the conductor

- **One master timeline,** scrubbed by scroll, to which everything else is subordinate: camera progress along the spline, chamber choreography, lighting and grade shifts. One timeline is the technical enforcement of “one continuous shot.”
- **Nested set-piece timelines** — the Assembly, the Understudy, the Beam, the Binding — pinned to dwell zones with labeled beats, so their internal drama (gather, hold, release) is choreographed once and scrubbed smoothly at any visitor speed.
- **Scrub smoothing** of roughly a second between the visitor's scroll and the camera's response: the source of the cinematic, weighty feel — the world has mass and follows, rather than twitching.
- **The ease vocabulary:** long exponential arrivals, sine-based breathing, nothing bouncy in the world — the machine is precise, and its easing says so. Springs are reserved for the interface layer.
- **Velocity shaping** for the ninety-second cut: travel zones compress under fast scroll while dwell zones play abridged-but-complete, preserving narrative order at any speed.

### Framer Motion — the visitor's hands

- **Placard choreography:** wall text enters as staggered mask-lifts, line by line, timed to camera arrival — typesetting as motion.
- **Micro-interactions:** the magnetic Lens and instrument-panel actions, the optimization dial's detented click, glass plates lifting and tilting under hover — all on spring physics tuned soft and quick, so the interface feels hand-finished against the world's cinematic weight.
- **Layout morphs:** the chamber index unfolding from the instrument panel, the pricing plates re-sorting on selection — continuous shared-element transformations, never re-renders that blink.
- **State elegance:** hover, focus, and press states rendered as light (a hairline brightening, a caustic warming) rather than color swaps — focus visible enough for accessibility, beautiful enough for the world.

### The seam that must never show

The two render layers are one place. The presence model (§5) is shared; lighting is shared conceptually — when the world dims into the Core, the placard layer's ink and paper follow the same grade; when a DOM element is hovered, nearby world-light responds. The visitor must never be able to tell where the canvas ends and the document begins.

### Loading & streaming

The First Breath is the loading strategy: a minimal payload boots the mote field and typography immediately; chambers stream in ahead of the camera; heavy garment textures resolve from mote-clusters — assets *arriving as light*, diegetically. No spinner exists anywhere in the experience.

## 9 · The Emotional Arc Map

The journey engineered as feeling. The right-hand column is the design's actual target: the sentence forming in the visitor's head.

| Chamber | Feeling | Mechanism | The visitor's inner monologue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Threshold | Curiosity | A living field that notices the cursor; one line of type | “What is this?” |
| The Listening | Wonder | Their own world — garments, orders — flowing as light | “Oh — it's reading a store.” |
| Paradox Gallery | Insight | Being taught a true, counterintuitive thing about their business | “Wait. That's true about *my* store.” |
| The Core | Awe | Scale, darkness, chaos becoming order around them | “This is the intelligence.” |
| Composition Hall | Understanding | The brief assembling piece by visible piece | “It actually builds the whole thing.” |
| The Range | Confidence | Deliberate aim, then results as inscriptions; honest sourcing | “The numbers are real — and it aims first.” |
| Observatory | Trust | The whole organism at once; their traces remembered; stated exclusions | “This thing is alive, and it's not for everyone.” |
| Invitation | Desire → action | The world itself flowing into one indigo point | “Point it at mine.” |

Two structural notes. The arc deliberately places **insight before awe** — the visitor is taught something true *before* they meet the Core, so the machine's spectacle is read as competence rather than decoration. And trust is built by **restraint stacked on proof**: pilot-brand sourcing, stated exclusions, session-only memory — three small honesties that together do more than any testimonial wall.

## 10 · Beyond the Desktop

**Touch.** The journey is identical in structure; the hands change. Lensing becomes touch: the world leans toward a resting finger, and a slow press is the “attend” gesture that parts the Shoal or lifts a plate. Device tilt (opt-in) adds a breath of parallax. Chamber lengths retune for thumb-scroll rhythm, and the Paradox Gallery restages vertically — bestseller above, understudy below, the spotlight swinging down the page. Dwell set-pieces play from the same choreography, reframed portrait.

**The Still Edition.** Reduced motion is a designed sibling, not a degraded fallback. The journey becomes a sequence of exquisite editorial plates — the machine *photographed* rather than filmed: the Core mid-inhale, the spotlight mid-swing, the Brief bound — with the identical copy deck, gentle crossfades only, and full information parity down to the pricing drawer. It triggers on the visitor's reduced-motion preference and from a visible “Still” toggle in the instrument panel; it is also the no-WebGL fallback and a print-worthy artifact in its own right. It should be beautiful enough that some visitors choose it.

**Sound (optional, off by default).** A generative ambience derived from a single musical scale, so the machine hums in key: filtered air in the white chambers, a low warm pulse from the Core, small glass chimes on assembly events, the faintest rising tone as the Gravity Well gathers. One-tap toggle in the instrument panel, state remembered. Never autoplayed, never necessary — the piece is complete silent.

**Small screens.** The same world through a re-authored portrait camera path — tighter framing, lower mote budgets, post stack trimmed. Nothing is hidden on mobile; it is reframed.

## 11 · Craft & Performance Standards

The experience claims elite engineering; it must therefore *be* elite engineering. These standards are part of the creative direction, because a dropped frame is an aesthetic failure.

- **Frame budget:** 60fps on mid-tier consumer hardware. Three device tiers scale the world — mote populations from roughly eight to sixty thousand, post stack from grade-only up to the full editorial set — chosen by capability probe, adjustable by hand, and thermally aware on mobile (sustained-load tier-down before the fan does the reviewing).
- **Weight:** a strict initial payload behind the First Breath; everything else streams ahead of the camera. Garment photography atlased and compressed to the edge of visible loss; a total-experience weight ceiling agreed in week one and defended thereafter.
- **Accessibility as a first-class edition:** full keyboard traversal using the chamber index as landmarks; focus rendered as light but meeting contrast requirements; all placard text real DOM text — selectable, indexable, translatable; a screen-reader narrative built from the copy deck plus scene descriptions, so the journey is *narrated* rather than skipped; the Still Edition guaranteeing full parity of information and offer.
- **Longevity:** the five-year bet is structural. New product capabilities join as new tributaries or new artifacts from the Core — the world absorbs features without redesign. The palette and type system are editorial rather than trendy, chosen to age like a magazine, not a launch page. Seasonal art swaps (new garment archives) refresh the surface without touching the machine.

## 12 · Appendix — Production Roadmap

Twenty-six weeks, US $500,000. The plan spends disproportionately on look-development and tuning, because that is where “unforgettable” actually lives.

| Phase | Weeks | Work | Gate to pass |
|---|---|---|---|
| Narrative lock | 1–3 | Script table-read of Section 3; boards for all fourteen moments; copy deck frozen | The story works read aloud, with no pictures |
| World-building | 3–8 | Art direction; look-dev of the Core, motes, glass, and grade in isolation; type and palette lock; standalone motion prototypes of the four riskiest moments — Through the Glass, the Assembly, the Shoal, the Gravity Well | **The eight-second test:** cold viewers shown the look-dev reel say “intelligent / precise” unprompted within eight seconds |
| Production | 8–18 | Camera spline authoring; chambers built hardest-story-first (Composition Hall → Core → Paradox Gallery → remainder); placard layer; living systems; touch edition | Full journey traversable end to end at target frame rate on tier-two hardware |
| The tuning pass | 18–22 | Easing, dwell lengths, lens intensities, type sizes, beat timing; moderated user tests scored against the §9 inner-monologue targets | Test visitors hit at least six of the eight monologue beats unprompted |
| Hardening & launch | 22–26 | Device matrix, Still Edition completion, accessibility audit, performance certification, content freeze, launch | Every standard in §11 passes on the full matrix |

**Team shape (core, ~9):** creative director · art director · two creative technologists (world and shaders) · interaction designer · motion designer · producer · copy-level editor (part-time — twelve lines deserve a professional) · QA/accessibility specialist.

**Budget posture:** roughly 20% concept and look-development, 55% production, 20% tuning and hardening, 5% contingency. The tuning allocation is protected; it is the difference between a good site and the one people send each other.
